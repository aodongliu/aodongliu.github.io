Stranger Stats #3: He Shot 0/17 In an NBA Game, and 30 Years Later His Son Shot 0/12?

While watching Lakers vs. Bucks on the night of January 15, 2026, I witnessed two rough shooting lines unfold in real time: Kevin Porter Jr. went 0-for-8 for the Bucks, and Gabe Vincent finished 0-for-7 for the Lakers. Tough nights, no doubt.

But have you wondered who in the past has an even worse shooting performance? Let StrangerStats take you on a journey to uncover some of the worst “0/X field goal attempts” games ever recorded.

1) Most field-goal attempts without a field goal

There are bad shooting nights… and then there are nights where you miss so many shots that it becomes a trivia question. The title for most field goal attempts without a single bucket belongs to Tim Hardaway Sr.. In 1991 he shot 0 for 17 in their win against the Timberwolves, and the record has stood for 35 years (and counting…).

Tim Hardaway
1991-12-27 — Golden State Warriors @ Minnesota Timberwolves (W)
FG 0/17 · 3P 0/2 · FT 2/2 · 2 PTS · 44 MIN
REB 3 AST 13 STL 2 BLK 1 TOV 4 PF 5 +/- +0

It was quite amazing that the Warriors were able to stil come away with a W! Despite his horrible shooting performance, Tim Hardaway Sr. dished out 13 assists and chipped in for the victory. His hall-of-fame teammate Chris Mullin did the heavy lifting. He shot 15 for 24 and scored 36 points, and covered for Hardaway’s historically cold night.
Full box score on Basketball-Reference

The uncanny part is that 3 decades later, his son Tim Hardaway Jr. authored his own entry into this odd family trivia, shooting 0-for-12 from the field!! However, his team wasn’t so lucky, as they got blown out by the Toronto Raptors. Full box score on Basketball-Reference

Tim Hardaway Jr.
2021-01-18 — Dallas Mavericks @ Toronto Raptors (L)
FG 0/12 · 3P 0/6 · FT 0/0 · 0 PTS · 26 MIN
REB 5 AST 1 STL 0 BLK 0 TOV 2 PF 1 +/- -12

3) Most points scored with 0 made field goals

This is where things get weird.

How do you score a lot of points without making a single shot from the field?

Answer: free throws, and lots of them.

There you have it, one of the most weird games I found. Darrick Martin, who on this night could not make a single shot from the field (0/8 in FG), nailed all his free throws (15/15 in FT), making him the highest scorer without a single field goal bucket.

Darrick Martin
1996-12-14 — Los Angeles Clippers vs Sacramento Kings (W)
FG 0/8 · 3P 0/1 · FT 15/15 · 15 PTS · 32 MIN
REB 0 AST 7 STL 2 BLK 0 TOV 2 PF 4 +/- +20

4) The true nightmare: 0-for-X FG and 0-for-Y FT

Missing every field goal is bad.

Missing every free throw too? That’s “turn off the phone for 48 hours” bad.

It turns out Joel Embiid and Kyrie Irving can trauma-bond over this experience, as both of them had a brutal night like this early in their respective careers.
