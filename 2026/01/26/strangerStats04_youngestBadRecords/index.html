Stranger Stats #4: Too Young, Too Simple? Breaking Down the Youngest NBA Records Nobody Wants

Earlier this month, on January 7th, 2026, Ayden Heaven of Manchester United scored an own goal off an unfortunate deflection against Burnley, making him the youngest ever to score an Premier League own goal in Manchester United History, at 19 years and 107 days old.

Moments like this are brutal. It’s not the kind of record anyone wants attached to their name. But at the same time, simply being in position to set it is a testament to a player’s talent: a sign that they were asked and trusted to take on meaningful responsibility at a very young age, even if things didn’t go their way. I think Ayden is having a solid season, and I hope in the future he can grow into an important piece at the back line of Manchester United.

This got me thinking: does the NBA have its own version of such moments?

Sure it does!

Sit back and let Stranger Stats take you on a tour through some youngest record holders for some embarrasing records in NBA history.

Among these, you’ll find future stars who were working through early growth pains, as well as lottery-ticket players that some teams gambled on but were never able to cash in. Did they capitalize on their potential as they matured, or did they eventually become busts? You be the judge!
